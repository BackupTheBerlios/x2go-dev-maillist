This error occurs indeed if your password is not typed in correctly. I  
sometimes also get this error (no idea why...), even if I am really  
sure that I typed my pw in correctly... But this error should not be  
permanent and it occurs rather rarely.

On your x2goserver system take a look at /var/log/auth.log while you  
try to login.

As well the client as the x2goserver produces log output in your local  
and your remote home dir (~/.x2go). Also check output and information  
there...

> Ab und zu sehe ich noch das hier
> "
> NXPROXY - Version 3.4.0
>

> See http://www.nomachine.com/ for more information.
>
> Info: Proxy running in client mode with pid '25163'.
> Session: Starting session at 'Fri Aug 13 10:29:55 2010'.
> Info: Connecting to remote host 'localhost:30002'.
> Info: Connection to remote proxy 'localhost:30002' established.
> Error: The remote NX proxy closed the connection.
> Error: Failure negotiating the session in stage '7'.
> Error: Wrong version or invalid session authentication cookie.
> Session: Terminating session at 'Fri Aug 13 10:29:56 2010'.
> Session: Session terminated at 'Fri Aug 13 10:29:56 2010'.

Maybe you should clean up the sessions  
(C-<uid>-<displayno>-<long-alnum-string>) in your remote ~/.x2go dir.

In a nutshell:

   watch /var/log/auth.log
   clear local .x2go dir
   clear remote .x2go dir
   look at individual session logs in .x2go (remotely+locally)
   post your insights

Normal x2goserver-home works out of the box on Ubuntu karmic (my  
experience). Do you have a firewall running? Any other tweaks /  
differences from a standard ubuntu desktop?
