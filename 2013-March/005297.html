Forwarding/bouncing SPAM makes out of a SPAM mail a not-so-much-SPAM  
mail. Several characteristics change during mail forwarding, so that  
SpamAssassin thinks it's rather ham than spam.

The mail you sent got a Spam-Level of 2 (5 and greater is considered  
as SPAM). If you find a SPAM mail in the BTS, you can check the  
flagging by SpamAssassin yourself. Click on ,,full text'' above the  
header of each bug message.

There, the first lines show something like this:
"""
X-Spam-Checker-Version: SpamAssassin 3.3.2 (2011-06-06) on
	ymir.das-netzwerkteam.de
X-Spam-Level:
X-Spam-Status: No, score=0.0 required=5.0 tests=URIBL_BLOCKED autolearn=ham
	version=3.3.2
"""

And as always with a SpamAssassin installation: There is always a grey  
zone where SPAM is recognized as non-SPAM. And: the filter has to  
learn. Let's wait for some weeks and then the filtering will be close  
to 100%.
